1. How do I approach designing software?

   When I started building the candlestick for my project, I took it one shape at a time. I began at the bottom and worked my way up so I could get a solid foundation down before adding anything else. That way I could get a firm foundation in place and perfect it before adding another. I planned out which shapes I'd use before I even opened Visual Studio. Working on this project I realized that pretty much any object can be broken down into a bunch of simple shapes and stacked together. To get the sizing and positioning of the shapes just right, I mostly just used the shape made before it as a reference and then tried different numbers until it looked just right. I added one shape, tested it, and repeated that over and over. If something looked off, I adjusted the numbers until it matched my reference photo better. It was a lot of trial and error, but it taught me that getting things to look right just takes time and patience. In the future, I'll definitely break bigger problems down into smaller pieces and test as I go instead of trying to do everything at once.
